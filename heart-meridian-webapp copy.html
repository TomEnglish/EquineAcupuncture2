Heart Meridian (HT)

Overview

  • Origin: The Heart meridian begins in the heart
  • Trajectory: Travels through the chest, emerges at the axilla, flows along the caudomedial aspect of the forelimb
  • Endpoint: Terminates at the medial corner of the coronary band of the front hoof
  • Function: Governs blood circulation, consciousness, and mental-emotional states
  • Element: Fire

HT-1 (Jia-qi)

  • Location: Center of axillary space over transverse pectoral muscle between trunk and forelimb
  • Indications: Shoulder pain
Anatomical diagram showing HT-1 location in the axillary space

HT-3 (Shao-hai)

  • Location: Between olecranon and medial epicondyle of humerus, ventral to medial head of triceps
  • Notes: He-sea Point, Water point
  • Indications: Elbow pain, Chest pain
Anatomical diagram showing HT-3 location near the elbow

HT-4 (Ling-dao)

  • Location: Caudolateral border of forelimb, 1.5 cun proximal to HT-7 on caudal aspect of ulnaris lateralis m.
  • Notes: Jing-river (Metal)
  • Indications: Shen disturbance, insomnia, anxiety, restlessness
Anatomical diagram showing HT-4 location on the forelimb

HT-5 (Tong-li)

  • Location: Caudolateral border of forelimb, 1 cun proximal to HT-7 on caudal aspect of ulnaris lateralis muscle
  • Notes: Luo connecting point for SI
  • Indications: Insomnia, Restlessness, Anxiety
Anatomical diagram showing HT-5 location on the forearm

HT-6 (Yin-xi)

  • Location: Caudolateral border of forelimb, 0.5 cun proximal to HT-7
  • Notes: Xi-cleft point
  • Indications: Acute cardiac pain, Shen disturbance, Sweating due to Yin deficiency
Anatomical diagram showing HT-6 location on the forearm

HT-7 (Guo-liang)

  • Location: Caudolateral aspect of radius in depression on caudal aspect of ulnaris lateralis muscle, proximal to most proximal aspect of accessory carpal bone
  • Notes:
    • Use in fearful fire horse
    • Shu-stream point (Earth)
    • Yuan (primary) point
  • Indications: Palpitation, Shen disturbance, anxiety, restlessness, chest pain, manic
Anatomical diagram showing HT-7 location near the carpus

HT-8 (Shao-fu)

  • Location: Palmerolateral aspect of third metacarpal bone, half distance from carpus to fetlock between suspensory ligament and deep digital flexor tendon
  • Notes: Ying-spring point (Fire)
  • Indications: Genital itching, Urinary difficulty or incontinence
Anatomical diagram showing HT-8 location on the metacarpus

HT-9 (Shao-chong)

  • Location: Palmarilateral aspect of thoracic hoof, 0.5 cun proximal to coronary band
  • Notes: Jing-well point, Mother point
  • Indications: Chest pain, Shoulder pain, Front limb lameness, Laminitis, Coma, Shen disturbance
Anatomical diagram showing HT-9 location near the coronary band

Heart Meridian - Five-Element Points

Point Element Type Primary Function
HT-9 Wood Jing-well Powerful for acute conditions
HT-8 Fire Ying-spring Treats Heat conditions
HT-7 Earth Shu-stream Yuan source point
HT-4 Metal Jing-river Treats Qi-level issues
HT-3 Water He-sea Treats chronic conditions

Heart Meridian - Clinical Applications

  • Cardiac function: HT-7, HT-9
  • Mental-emotional issues: HT-7, HT-5, HT-4
  • Limb pain/lameness: HT-9, HT-3
  • Febrile conditions: HT-8, HT-6
  • Urogenital disorders: HT-8

Heart Meridian - Treatment Combinations

  • For Shen disturbance: HT-7 + PC-6
  • For chest pain: HT-3 + CV-17
  • For forelimb issues: HT-9 + LI-4
  • For anxiety/stress: HT-7 + GV-20 + An-shen (174)
  • For cardiac support: HT-7 + BL-15 (Back-shu for Heart)
