Large Intestine Meridian (LI)

Overview

  • Origin: The Large Intestine meridian begins at the craniomedial aspect of the forelimb just proximal to the coronary band (LI-1)
  • Trajectory: Travels up the craniomedial aspect of the forelimb, crosses the carpus to the craniolateral forelimb, up the neck and then to the face
  • Endpoint: Terminates lateral to the nostrils (LI-20)
  • Function: Governs elimination and detoxification
  • Note: Does not cross over any other meridians

LI-1 (Shang-yang)

  • Location: Dorsomedial aspect of the thoracic hoof, at the origin of the medial collateral ligament of the distal interphalangeal joint, 0.5 cun proximal to coronary band
  • Notes:
    • Centered over MCL of coffin joint
    • Jing-well point (Metal)
  • Indications: Nasal discharge, not wanting to be the leader in a race, shoulder pain or lameness, forelimb lameness, navicular disease, laminitis, side-bone, ring-bone, pharyngitis
Anatomical diagram showing LI-1 location at the dorsomedial aspect of the thoracic hoof

LI-2 (Er-jian)

  • Location: On the dorsomedial side of the thoracic limb, distal to the metacarpophalangeal joint, in the depression dorsal to the junction of the medial extensor branch of the suspensory ligament and the common digital extensor tendon
  • Notes: Ying-spring point (Water)
  • Indications: LI Heat, fever, diarrhea, nose-bleeding
Anatomical diagram showing LI-2 location on the dorsomedial side of the thoracic limb

LI-3 (San-jian)

  • Location: On the dorsomedial aspect of the thoracic limb, just distal to the distal end of the 2nd metacarpal bone, dorsal to the suspensory ligament
  • Notes: Shu-stream point (Wood)
  • Indications: Pain in the fetlock, tendonitis, dental pain, diarrhea, abdominal fullness
Anatomical diagram showing LI-3 location on the dorsomedial aspect of the thoracic limb

LI-4 (He-gu)

  • Location: Distal and dorsal to the base of the 2nd metacarpal bone, in the depression between the 2nd and 3rd metacarpal bones
  • Notes:
    • Master Point for face and mouth
    • Yuan primary point
    • +LIV-3 = 4 gates
  • Indications: Facial paralysis, dental problems, nosebleed, sore throat, anhidrosis, tendonitis, fever, immunodeficiency
Anatomical diagram showing LI-4 location in the depression between the 2nd and 3rd metacarpal bones

LI-10 (Qian-san-li)

  • Location: 2 cun distal to LI-11, in the groove between the extensor carpi radialis and the common digital muscles
  • Notes:
    • Groove more obvious with leg raised
    • Thoracic three mile point
  • Indications: Diarrhea, colic, front limb paralysis, pain in dentals and cheek, general weakness. Substitute for ST-36
Anatomical diagram showing LI-10 location in the groove between muscles

LI-11 (Qu-chi)

  • Location: In a depression cranial to the elbow (lateral aspect), in the groove between the extensor carpi radialis and the common digital extensor just ventral to the lateral head of the triceps muscle
  • Notes: He-sea point (Earth), mother point
  • Indications: Sore throat, dental pain, uveitis, fever, hypertension, seizure, abdominal pain, diarrhea, front limb paralysis
Anatomical diagram showing LI-11 location cranial to the elbow

LI-15 (Jian-jing)

  • Location: Dorsal to the greater tubercle of the humerus, lateral to the tendon of origin of the biceps brachii, between the subclavius and the supraspinatus muscles
  • Notes:
    • Updated location
    • Can tent skin
  • Indications: Shoulder lameness
Anatomical diagram showing LI-15 location at the shoulder area

LI-18 (Fu-tu)

  • Location: On the ventral aspect of the brachiocephalic muscle at the level of C2-C3, dorsal to the jugular groove
  • Notes:
    • Lift head parallel to ground and trace line from ramus toward jugular groove
    • Diagnosis point for hoof problem
    • No one wants an 18yo horse with large fu-tu problems
  • Indications: Laryngeal hemiplegia, sore throat, thyroid disorders, cough, asthma
Anatomical diagram showing LI-18 location at the neck area

LI-20 (Ying-xiang)

  • Location: Along the lateral edge of nares, 2/3 of the way from the rostral to the dorsal edge, 1 cun caudal to the nares
  • Indications: Nasal congestion and discharge, facial paralysis, nose bleeding, facial itching, urticaria, sunstroke, cough, sinusitis, fever, cold
  • Special Use: Bleed this point for hemorrhagic diarrhea
Anatomical diagram showing LI-20 location at the lateral edge of nares

Large Intestine Meridian - Five-Element Points

Point Element Type Primary Function
LI-1 Metal Jing-well Powerful for acute conditions
LI-2 Water Ying-spring Treats Heat conditions
LI-3 Wood Shu-stream Treats chronic conditions
LI-5 Fire Jing-river Treats Qi-level issues
LI-11 Earth He-sea Mother point, treats chronic conditions

Large Intestine Meridian - Clinical Applications

  • Facial and dental issues: LI-4, LI-18, LI-20
  • Respiratory problems: LI-18, LI-20
  • Forelimb lameness: LI-1, LI-15
  • Digestive issues: LI-10, LI-11
  • Fever and immune function: LI-4, LI-11
  • Diagnostic points: LI-18 (hoof problems), LI-17 (carpal pain)
