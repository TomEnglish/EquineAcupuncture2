Small Intestine Meridian (SI)

Overview

  • Origin: The Small Intestine meridian begins at the lateral coronary band of the forelimb
  • Trajectory: Travels up the lateral aspect of the limb to the scapula, continues to the neck and face
  • Endpoint: Terminates at the ear
  • Function: Aids in the separation of clear from turbid substances, supports mental clarity, and influences hearing
  • Element: Fire

SI-1 (Shao-ze)

  • Location: Dorsolateral aspect of the thoracic hoof, 0.5 cun proximal to the coronary band
  • Notes: Jing-well point (Metal)
  • Indications: Laminitis, sore throat, mastitis, fever, coma, shoulder pain
Anatomical diagram showing SI-1 location near the coronary band

SI-2 (Qian-gu)

  • Location: Lateral aspect of the pastern, in the depression distal to the metacarpophalangeal joint, palmar to the extensor branch of suspensory ligament
  • Notes: Ying-spring point (Water)
  • Indications: Sore throat, otitis, lack of milk yield, febrile disease, eye pain
Anatomical diagram showing SI-2 location on the lateral aspect of the pastern

SI-3 (Qian-chan-wan)

  • Location: In a depression proximolateral to the metacarpophalangeal joint, on the palmarolateral aspect of the 3rd metacarpal, just distal to the end of the 4th metacarpal, dorsal to the suspensory ligament
  • Notes: Shu-stream point (Wood-mother)
  • Indications: Contusion, sprain, swelling of the fetlock, tendinitis of flexor tendons, cervical stiffness, back pain, sore throat, shoulder lameness, laminitis, seizure, manic
Anatomical diagram showing SI-3 location near the metacarpophalangeal joint

SI-4 (Wan-gu)

  • Location: Distal and dorsal to the base of the 4th metacarpal bone, in the depression between the 3rd and 4th metacarpal bones (opposite to LI-4)
  • Notes: Yuan primary point
  • Indications: Cervical stiffness, jaundice, fever, carpal pain and arthritis
Anatomical diagram showing SI-4 location between the 3rd and 4th metacarpal bones

SI-5 (Yang-gu)

  • Location: Distal and dorsal to the base of the 4th metacarpal bone, in the depression between the 3rd and 4th metacarpal bones (opposite to LI-4)
  • Notes: Jing-river point (Fire)
  • Indications: Epilepsy, stomatitis, ulceration in the mouth, halitosis, chest pain
Anatomical diagram showing SI-5 location between the 3rd and 4th metacarpal bones

SI-6 (Zhi-gou)

  • Location: 4 cun proximal to the antebrachiocarpalarticulation, caudal to the common digital extensor m.
  • Notes: Jing-river point (Fire)
  • Indications: Constipation, febrile diseases, ear problems
Anatomical diagram showing SI-6 location proximal to the antebrachiocarpalarticulation

SI-7 (Hui-zong)

  • Location: 4 cun proximal to the antebrachiocarpalarticulation, caudal to the tendon of the lateral digital extensor m.
  • Notes: Xi-cleft point
  • Indications: Acute epilepsy, deafness, paralysis
Anatomical diagram showing SI-7 location proximal to the antebrachiocarpalarticulation

SI-8 (San-yang-luo)

  • Location: 5 cun proximal to the antebrachiocarpalarticulation, caudal to the common digital extensor m.
  • Notes: General analgesia point
Anatomical diagram showing SI-8 location proximal to the antebrachiocarpalarticulation

SI-9 (Qian-jiu)

  • Location: On the palmar midline of the thoracic limb, in a depression dorsal to the bulbs of the heel
  • Notes: Jing-well point (Wood)
  • Indications: Hyperactivity, Shen disturbance, anhidrosis, superficial tendon problem, laminitis, navicular disease
Anatomical diagram showing SI-9 location on the palmar midline of the thoracic limb

SI-10 (Nao-shu)

  • Location: Caudal margin of the scapulohumeral joint, in a depression when the leg is lifted
  • Indications: Shoulder lameness
Anatomical diagram showing SI-10 location at the caudal margin of the scapulohumeral joint

SI-11 (Tian-zong)

  • Location: In the center of the infraspinatus muscle, in the depression halfway between the dorsal and ventral borders of the scapula
  • Indications: Shoulder pain, mastitis, dyspnea, thoracic pain
Anatomical diagram showing SI-11 location in the center of the infraspinatus muscle

SI-12 (Bing-feng)

  • Location: In the depression at the center of the supraspinatus muscle, between the scapular spine and the cranial border of the scapula
  • Indications: Pain in shoulder and thorax, mastitis, dyspnea, hemiplegia
Anatomical diagram showing SI-12 location in the depression at the center of the supraspinatus muscle

SI-16 (Tian-chuang)

  • Location: In the depression of the cervical vertebra, at the level of the cranial edge of the cutaneous colli muscle
  • Indications: Neck pain, ear problems, laryngeal hemiplegia, pharyngitis, epistaxis, rhinitis
Anatomical diagram showing SI-16 location in the depression of the cervical vertebra

SI-17 (Tian-rong)

  • Location: In the depression caudal to the border of the jaw, in the groove of the larynx and mandibular gland
  • Indications: Laryngeal hemiplegia, ear problems
Anatomical diagram showing SI-17 location in the depression caudal to the border of the jaw

SI-19 (Ting-gong)

  • Location: In the depression found anterior to the tragus when the mouth is open
  • Indications: Ear problems, TMJ pain, tinnitus, deafness
Anatomical diagram showing SI-19 location in the depression anterior to the tragus
