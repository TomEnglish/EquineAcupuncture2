Bladder Meridian (BL)

The Bladder meridian is the longest meridian in the body. It begins at the medial canthus of the eye (BL-1), runs up to the earbase, the atlas, then to the withers, then continues down the back in two parallel lines all the way to the lateral aspect of the hind foot.

Large Intestine Meridian (LI)

The Large Intestine meridian begins at the craniomedial aspect of the forelimb just proximal to the coronary band, travels up the craniomedial aspect of the forelimb, crosses the carpus to the craniolateral forelimb, up the neck and then to the face.

View Large Intestine Meridian

Lung Meridian (LU)

The Lung meridian begins in the middle jiao (stomach), emerges at LU-1 in the center of the pectorals, then travels abaxially to the cubital crease to LU-5, follows the craniomedial aspect of the radius, then passes caudal to the carpus and metacarpus.

View Lung Meridian

Stomach Meridian (ST)

The Stomach meridian begins just ventral to the middle of the eye (ST-1), courses through the face and neck, traverses the abdomen, then descends along the craniolateral aspect of the hind limb to end at the dorsal midline just proximal to the coronet (ST-45).

View Stomach Meridian

Governing Vessel (GV)

The Governing Vessel (Du Mai) runs along the dorsal midline of the body from the perineum, up the spine, over the top of the head, and ends at the upper lip. As one of the Eight Extraordinary Vessels, it's known as the "Sea of Yang".

View Governing Vessel

Conception Vessel (CV)

The Conception Vessel (Ren Mai) runs along the ventral midline of the body from the perineum, through the abdomen and chest, and ends at the lower lip. As one of the Eight Extraordinary Vessels, it's known as the "Sea of Yin".

View Conception Vessel

Kidney Meridian (KID)

The Kidney meridian originates in the kidneys and travels through the lower body, along the medial aspect of the hindlimb. It governs water metabolism, growth, reproduction, and development. The Kidney is associated with the Water element.

View Kidney Meridian

Spleen Meridian (SP)

The Spleen meridian begins at the medial aspect of the hind hoof, travels up the medial aspect of the distal hind limb, and terminates at the 10th ICS at the level of the point of the shoulder. It governs transformation and transportation of nutrients.

View Spleen Meridian

Triple Heater Meridian (TH)

The Triple Heater meridian begins at the lateral aspect of the ring finger, runs up the dorsolateral aspect of the forelimb to the shoulder, then along the neck to the face, ending at the lateral aspect of the eyebrow.

View Triple Heater Meridian

Small Intestine Meridian (SI)

The Small Intestine meridian begins at the lateral coronary band of the forelimb, travels up the lateral aspect of the limb to the scapula, continues to the neck and face, and terminates at the ear. It aids in the separation of clear from turbid substances.

View Small Intestine Meridian

Gallbladder Meridian (GB)

The Gallbladder meridian begins at the outer canthus of the eye, traverses the lateral aspect of the head and neck, passes through the shoulder region, descends along the lateral aspect of the trunk and hindlimb, and terminates at the fourth toe.

View Gallbladder Meridian

Liver Meridian (LIV)

The Liver meridian originates at the lateral aspect of the big toe, ascends along the medial aspect of the leg and thigh, enters the inguinal region, passes through the abdomen and hypochondrium, and ends at the vertex of the ribcage.

View Liver Meridian
