Gallbladder Meridian (GB)

Overview

  • Origin: The Gallbladder meridian begins at the outer canthus of the eye
  • Trajectory: Traverses the lateral aspect of the head and neck, passes through the shoulder region, descends along the lateral aspect of the trunk and hindlimb
  • Endpoint: Terminates at the fourth toe
  • Function: Aids in decision-making, regulates tendons and joints, and supports bile production
  • Element: Wood

GB-1 (Tai-yang)

  • Location: 1 cun caudoventral to the lateral canthus of the eye, over the transverse facial vein
  • Indications: Eye problems, anhidrosis, colds
Anatomical diagram showing GB-1 location caudoventral to the lateral canthus of the eye

GB-3 (Shang-guan)

  • Location: In the depression at the caudodorsal aspect of the TMJ, dorsal to the zygomatic arch of the temporal bone (The depression is easily seen when the horse opens its mouth)
  • Indications: Facial paralysis, syncope, Wind pattern, Shen disturbances
Anatomical diagram showing GB-3 location at the caudodorsal aspect of the TMJ

GB-20 (Feng-chi)

  • Location: Just cranial to the atlas, two cun lateral to the dorsal midline
  • Indications: External Wind, Internal Wind, cervical stiffness, nose-bleeding, nasal discharge/congestion, epilepsy
Anatomical diagram showing GB-20 location cranial to the atlas

GB-21 (Bo-zhong)

  • Location: In a depression at the midpoint of the cranial edge of the scapula, caudal to the subclavius m.
  • Indications: Shoulder pain, forelimbs paralysis, mastitis, difficult labor
Anatomical diagram showing GB-21 location at the midpoint of the cranial edge of the scapula

GB-24 (Ri-yue)

  • Location: 14th intercostal space at the level of the elbow, caudal to LIV-14
  • Notes: Alarm point for GB
  • Indications: Hepatic diseases, Liver Qi Stagnation
Anatomical diagram showing GB-24 location at the 14th intercostal space

GB-25 (Jing-men)

  • Location: In a depression halfway between the caudal border of the costochondral junction of the 18th rib and lumbar muscles
  • Notes: Alarm point for Kidney
  • Indications: Infertility, ovary disorders, lumbar pain, colic, impaction, Kidney Deficiency
Anatomical diagram showing GB-25 location between the costochondral junction and lumbar muscles

GB-27 (Wu-shu)

  • Location: 0.5 cun craniodorsal to the craniodorsal aspect of the tuber coxa
  • Notes: Diagnostic point for hock pain
  • Indications: Back pain, poor performance, hock pain
Anatomical diagram showing GB-27 location craniodorsal to the tuber coxa

GB-29 (Ju-liao)

  • Location: In a depression midway between tuber coxa and greater trochanter of the femur
  • Indications: Poor performance, gluteal muscle soreness, hindlimb pain, coxofemoral joint arthritis, hindlimb paralysis
Anatomical diagram showing GB-29 location between tuber coxa and greater trochanter

GB-30 (Huan-tiao)

  • Location: In a depression, on the caudoventral margin of the greater trochanter
  • Indications: Same as GB-29: Poor performance, gluteal muscle soreness, hindlimb pain, coxofemoral joint arthritis, hindlimb paralysis
Anatomical diagram showing GB-30 location on the caudoventral margin of the greater trochanter

GB-34 (Yang-ling-quan)

  • Location: Craniodistal to the head of fibula, in the interosseous space between the tibia and fibula, between the long and lateral digital extensor mm.
  • Notes: He-sea point (Earth), Influential point for tendon and ligaments
  • Indications: Weakness of tendon/ligament, rear weakness
Anatomical diagram showing GB-34 location craniodistal to the head of fibula

GB-35 (Yang-jiao)

  • Location: 1 cun caudal to GB-36
  • Notes: Xi-cleft point for Yang-Wei
  • Indications: Acute fullness of the chest
Anatomical diagram showing GB-35 location caudal to GB-36

GB-36 (Wai-qiu)

  • Location: 2 cun proximal to GB-37 (or 7 cun proximal to the lateral malleolus), between the lateral digital extensor m. and the deep digital flexor m.
  • Notes: Xi-cleft point for GB
  • Indications: Liver Yang rising (anger, irritability, hypertension)
Anatomical diagram showing GB-36 location between the lateral digital extensor and deep digital flexor muscles

GB-39 (Xuan-zhong)

  • Location: 3 cun proximal to the tip of the lateral malleolus, on the caudal aspect of the tibia, between the lateral digital extensor m. and the deep digital flexor m.
  • Notes: Influential point for marrow
  • Indications: Cervical stiffness, hindlimb paresis or paralysis, anus problems, chest pain, sore throat
Anatomical diagram showing GB-39 location proximal to the lateral malleolus

GB-40 (Qiu-xu)

  • Location: Cranial to BL-62. At the distal border of the lateral malleolus of the tibia
  • Notes: Yuan primary point
  • Indications: Pain in hock, chest pain
Anatomical diagram showing GB-40 location at the distal border of the lateral malleolus

GB-41 (Zu-lin-qi)

  • Location: Distal and dorsal to the base of the 4th metatarsal bone, in the depression between the 4th and 3rd metatarsal bones
  • Notes: Shu-stream point (Wood), Confluent point with Dai Channel
  • Indications: Pain in metatarsal region, flexor tendonitis, urinary incontinence, abnormal cycling, eye problems, mastitis
Anatomical diagram showing GB-41 location between the 4th and 3rd metatarsal bones

GB-44 (Zu-qiao-yin)

  • Location: Dorsolateral aspect of the pelvic limb, 0.5 cun proximal to the coronary band
  • Notes: Jing-well point (Metal)
  • Indications: Laminitis, hock pain, hip problems, coxa problem, abnormal cycling, laminitis
Anatomical diagram showing GB-44 location proximal to the coronary band
