Triple Heater Meridian (TH)

Overview

  • Origin: The Triple Heater meridian begins at the lateral aspect of the ring finger
  • Trajectory: Runs up the dorsolateral aspect of the forelimb to the shoulder, then along the neck to the face
  • Endpoint: Ends at the lateral aspect of the eyebrow
  • Function: Regulates the functions of the three "burning spaces" (upper, middle, and lower jiao) and the movement of fluids throughout the body
  • Element: Fire
  • Also known as: Triple Burner or San Jiao

TH-10 (Tian-jing)

  • Location: 1 cun cranial to the dorsal aspect of the olecranon, in the lateral head of the triceps muscle, dorsocaudal to SI-8
  • Notes: He-sea point (Earth)
  • Indications: Forelimbs paralysis, sore throat, deafness, toothache
Anatomical diagram showing TH-10 location in the lateral head of the triceps muscle

TH-14 (Jian-wai-yu)

  • Location: Caudal margin of the scapulohumeral joint, between the insertions of the infraspinatus and the deltoideus mm.
  • Indications: Pain in shoulder and front limb
Anatomical diagram showing TH-14 location at the caudal margin of the scapulohumeral joint

TH-15 (Bo-jian)

  • Location: In the depression on craniodorsal border of scapula at the ventral edge of the rhomboideus muscle
  • Indications: Pain in shoulder and front limb, sore foot, cervical stiffness, suprascapular nerve paralysis, suspensory ligament problems
Anatomical diagram showing TH-15 location on the craniodorsal border of scapula

TH-16 (Tian-you)

  • Location: Dorsal border of the brachiocephalicus muscle, at the level of the articulation between the 1st and 2nd cervical vertebra, cranial and dorsal to SI-16
  • Notes: Diagnosis point for fetlock
  • Indications: Cervical stiffness, head-shaking, deafness, pain in temporomandibular joint (TMJ), wobbler syndrome
Anatomical diagram showing TH-16 location at the dorsal border of the brachiocephalicus muscle

TH-23 (Si-zhu-kong)

  • Location: Dorsal to the lateral canthus of the eye, caudal to orbit, over the zygomatic process of the frontal bone
  • Indications: Eye problems, facial paralysis, dental disease, encephalitis and epilepsy
