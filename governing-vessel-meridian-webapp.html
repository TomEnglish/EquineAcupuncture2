Governing Vessel Meridian (GV)

Overview

  • Origin: The Governing Vessel (Du Mai) begins at the perineum
  • Trajectory: Runs along the dorsal midline of the body, up the spine, over the top of the head
  • Endpoint: Terminates at the upper lip
  • Function: Known as the "Sea of Yang" and governs all Yang meridians, the brain, spinal cord, and the posterior-lateral aspects of the body
  • Classification: One of the Eight Extraordinary Vessels

GV-1 (Hou-yin)

  • Location: On the midline, in the depression between the anus and the ventral aspect of the tail
  • Notes: Luo-connecting point for CV meridian
  • Indications: Diarrhea, bloody defecation, constipation, perianal problems, seizure

GV-3 (Yao-yang-guan)

  • Location: In a depression on the dorsal midline, between the dorsal processes of L4 and L5
  • Indications: Coldness and pain in the back, lack of libido, hindlimb paralysis, Kidney Yang/Qi Deficiency
Anatomical diagram showing GV-3 location on the dorsal midline

GV-4 (Ming-men)

  • Location: In a depression on the dorsal midline, between the dorsal processes of L2 and L3
  • Indications: Yang Deficiency, lack of libido, abnormal cycling, diarrhea, back pain
Anatomical diagram showing GV-4 location on the dorsal midline

GV-5 (Xuan-shu)

  • Location: In a depression on the dorsal midline, between the dorsal processes of L1 and L2
  • Indications: Diarrhea, colic, back pain, hemorrhage
Anatomical diagram showing GV-5 location on the dorsal midline

GV-14 (Da-zhui)

  • Location: In the depression on the dorsal midline at the level of the cervicothoracic intervertebral space (C7-T1)
  • Indications: Fever, cough, heaves, sweat, cervical stiffness, skin rash, seizure
Anatomical diagram showing GV-14 location at the cervicothoracic junction

GV-16 (Tian-men)

  • Location: In the depression on the dorsal midline, at the atlantooccipital joint, between the right and left GB-20
  • Notes: Use with great caution, only superficial insertion
  • Indications: Cervical stiffness, seizure, stroke
Anatomical diagram showing GV-16 location at the atlantooccipital joint

GV-20 (Bai-hui)

  • Location: On the dorsal midline, at the dorsocranial aspect of the nuchal crest
  • Indications: Seizure, insomnia, prolapse of anus
Anatomical diagram showing GV-20 location at the nuchal crest

GV-24 (Da-feng-men)

  • Location: On the dorsal midline at the rostral end of the forelock
  • Indications: Nasal congestion, seizure
Anatomical diagram showing GV-24 location at the forelock

GV-26 (Wai-chun-yin)

  • Location: On the dorsal midline, between the ventral limits of the nostrils
  • Indications: Facial paralysis, coma, back pain
Anatomical diagram showing GV-26 location between the nostrils

GV-28 (Yin-jiao)

  • Location: On the midline of the inner surface of upper lip, on the maxillary labial vein
  • Indications: Facial paralysis, tetanus, behavioral problems
Anatomical diagram showing GV-28 location on the inner surface of upper lip
