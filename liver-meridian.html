Liver Meridian (LIV)

Overview

  • Origin: The Liver meridian originates at the lateral aspect of the big toe
  • Trajectory: Ascends along the medial aspect of the leg and thigh, enters the inguinal region, passes through the abdomen and hypochondrium
  • Endpoint: Ends at the vertex of the ribcage
  • Function: Regulates the smooth flow of Qi, stores Blood, and maintains tendons and ligaments
  • Element: Wood

LIV-1 (Da-dun)

  • Location: Dorsomedial aspect of the thoracic hoof, at the origin of the medial collateral ligament of the distal interphalangeal joint, 0.5 cun proximal to coronary band
  • Notes: Jing-well point (Wood)
  • Indications: Laminitis, side-bones, hernia
Anatomical diagram showing LIV-1 location near the coronary band

LIV-2 (Xing-jian)

  • Location: On the dorsomedial side of the pelvic limb, distal to the metatarsophalangeal joint, dorsal to the junction of the medial extensor branch of the suspensory ligament and the common digital extensor tendon
  • Notes: Ying-spring point (Fire)
  • Indications: Liver Yang rising, eye problems, ring-bone, side-bone, laminitis, abnormal cycling
Anatomical diagram showing LIV-2 location distal to the metatarsophalangeal joint

LIV-3 (Tai-chong)

  • Location: Distal and dorsal to the base of the 2nd metatarsal bone, in the depression between the 2nd and 3rd metatarsal bones
  • Notes: Shu-stream point (Earth), Yuan primary point
  • Indications: Liver Yang rising, eye problems, tendonitis, hypertension, mastitis, abnormal cycling
Anatomical diagram showing LIV-3 location between the 2nd and 3rd metatarsal bones

LIV-4 (Zhong-feng)

  • Location: Dorsal aspect of the hock, on the saphenous vein, proximal to the cunean tendon
  • Notes: Jing-river point (Metal)
  • Indications: Anhidrosis, hock pain, swelling and pain of hindlimb
Anatomical diagram showing LIV-4 location on the dorsal aspect of the hock

LIV-5 (Li-gou)

  • Location: In a depression 5 cun proximal to the medial malleolus (or 2 cun proximal to SP-6), on the cranial border of the tibia
  • Notes: Luo-connecting point for GB
  • Indications: Urinary dribbling, urinary incontinence, paralysis of hindlimb
Anatomical diagram showing LIV-5 location proximal to the medial malleolus

LIV-6 (Zhong-du)

  • Location: In a depression 7 cun proximal to the medial malleolus, on the cranial border of the tibia (or 2 cun proximal to LIV-5)
  • Notes: Xi-cleft point
  • Indications: Hernia, uterine problems, colic
Anatomical diagram showing LIV-6 location on the cranial border of the tibia

LIV-8 (Qu-quan)

  • Location: In a depression 2 cun cranial to KID-10 on the ventral border of the gracilis m.
  • Notes: He-sea point (Water)
  • Indications: Male infertility, genital itching, urinary incontinence, abnormal cycling, pain in stifle
Anatomical diagram showing LIV-8 location on the ventral border of the gracilis muscle

LIV-13 (Zhang-men)

  • Location: At the distal end of the 18th rib
  • Notes: Influential point for Zang organs, Alarm point for SP
  • Indications: Abdominal fullness, colic, diarrhea, abdominal mass, muscle pain
Anatomical diagram showing LIV-13 location at the distal end of the 18th rib

LIV-14 (Qi-men)

  • Location: In the 13th intercostal space at the level of the elbow
  • Notes: Alarm point for Liver
  • Indications: Hepatic disorders, mastitis, pleuritis, chest pain, muscle pain
Anatomical diagram showing LIV-14 location in the 13th intercostal space
