Spleen Meridian (SP)

Overview

  • Origin: The Spleen meridian begins at the medial aspect of the hind hoof
  • Trajectory: Travels up the medial aspect of the distal hind limb, tracks cranial over the tibia, crosses the medial aspect of the stifle, emerges at the flank fold (SP-10) to the cranioventral tuber coxae (SP-11), travels to the craniodorsal tuber coxae (SP-13) before descending to mid flank and traveling cranially over the costochondral junction
  • Endpoint: Terminates at the 10th ICS at the level of the point of the shoulder (SP-21)
  • Function: Governs transformation and transportation of nutrients, blood production, and muscle strength
  • Element: Earth

SP-1 (Yin-bai)

  • Location: Plantaromedial aspect of the pelvic limb at the medial collateral ligament of the coffin joint, 0.5 cun proximal to the coronary band
  • Notes: Jing-well point (Wood)
  • Indications: Laminitis, bloody urination, bloody defecation, abdominal pain or fullness
Anatomical diagram showing SP-1 location near the coronary band

SP-2 (Da-du)

  • Location: Distal to the medial sesamoid bone, plantar to the interosseous m.
  • Notes: Ying-spring point (Fire-mother)
  • Indications: Diarrhea, constipation, fever
Anatomical diagram showing SP-2 location distal to the medial sesamoid bone

SP-3 (Tai-bai)

  • Location: On the plantaromedial aspect of the pelvic limb, just distal to the distal end of the 2nd metatarsal bone, plantar to the suspensory ligament
  • Notes: Shu-stream point (Earth), Yuan primary point
  • Indications: Colic, diarrhea, constipation, obesity, joint pain, perianal conditions, laminitis
Anatomical diagram showing SP-3 location distal to the 2nd metatarsal bone

SP-4 (Gong-sun)

  • Location: In a depression on the pelvic limb, plantarodistal to the base of the medial splint bone, dorsal to the DDFT, plantar to LIV-3
  • Notes: Luo-connecting point for SP meridian, Confluent point with Chong extraordinary channel
  • Indications: Gastric pain, colic, diarrhea, vomiting
Anatomical diagram showing SP-4 location in the depression plantarodistal to the medial splint bone

SP-5 (Shang-qiu)

  • Location: In a depression plantar to the medial malleolus of the tibia, plantar to LIV-4
  • Notes: Jing-river (Metal-son)
  • Indications: Abdominal pain, diarrhea, constipation, jaundice, hock pain
Anatomical diagram showing SP-5 location in the depression plantar to the medial malleolus

SP-6 (San-yin-jiao)

  • Location: 3 cun proximal to the medial malleolus, caudal to the tibia
  • Notes: Master point for Caudal Abdomen and urogenital tract
  • Indications: Diarrhea, genital discharge, promotion of parturition, infertility, paralysis of hindlimb, lack of libido, abnormal cycling, hernia, urinary incontinence, insomnia. Combine with SP-9 for lower jiao damp heat
Anatomical diagram showing SP-6 location 3 cun proximal to the medial malleolus

SP-8 (Di-ji)

  • Location: On the caudal border of the tibia, 3 cun distal to SP-9, cranial to the long digital flexor
  • Notes: Xi-cleft point
  • Indications: Colic, diarrhea, edema, abnormal cycling, difficulty in urination
Anatomical diagram showing SP-8 location on the caudal border of the tibia

SP-9 (Yin-ling-quan)

  • Location: In a depression at the level of the tibial tuberosity, 0.5 cun cranial to the saphenous vein, on the ventral border of the gracilis muscle, cranial to the saphenous v. (The "panty line")
  • Notes: He-sea point (Water)
  • Indications: Edema, diarrhea, jaundice, urinary difficulty or incontinence, stifle pain and arthritis
Anatomical diagram showing SP-9 location at the level of the tibial tuberosity

  • Notes: Sea of Blood

SP-11 (Ji-men or Dan-tian)

  • Location: In the depression 1.5 cun cranioventral to the ventral border of tuber coxa
  • Notes: "Dan" of Dan & Julia ("Julia" = ST-31, Dan leads, Julia follows)
  • Indications: Difficulty in urination, urinary incontinence
Anatomical diagram showing SP-11 location cranioventral to the tuber coxa

SP-12 (Chong-men)

  • Location: In a depression ventral to the curve of the cranial aspect of the tuber coxa, ventral to the tendon of the obliquus internus abdominus m. (Top of the flank fold cowlick)
  • Notes: Stifle performance point
  • Indications: Colic, hernia
Anatomical diagram showing SP-12 location ventral to the cranial aspect of the tuber coxa

SP-13 (Fu-she)

  • Location: 0.5 cun cranioventral to the craniodorsal aspect of the tuber coxae, dorsal to the tendon of the obliquus internus abdominus m.
  • Notes: Hock and stifle performance point
  • Indications: Colic, hernia
Anatomical diagram showing SP-13 location cranioventral to the tuber coxae

SP-21 (Da-bao)

  • Location: At the 10th ICS at the same level as the point of the shoulder ("John's big hug" - Sore all over- needs a hug)
  • Notes: SP major Luo
  • Indications: Heaves, pain in entire body, Wei syndrome
