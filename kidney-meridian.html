Kidney Meridian (KID)

Overview

  • Origin: The Kidney meridian originates in the kidneys
  • Trajectory: Travels through the lower body, along the medial aspect of the hindlimb
  • Function: Governs water metabolism, growth, reproduction, and development
  • Element: Water

KID-3 (Tai-xi)

  • Location: Between the medial malleolus of the tibia and the calcaneal tuber, dorsal to the tarsal sheath
  • Notes: Shu-stream point (Earth), Yuan primary point
  • Indications: Abnormal cycling, dysuria, renal failure, diabetes, infertility, lack of libido, heaves, sore throat, toothache, back pain, ear problems
Anatomical diagram showing KID-3 location between the medial malleolus and calcaneal tuber

KID-4 (Da-zhong)

  • Location: 0.5 cun plantarodistal to KID-3 on the medial border of the calcaneus
  • Notes: Luo-connecting point with BL Meridian
  • Indications: Urinary incontinence, renal failure, heaves, hock pain
Anatomical diagram showing KID-4 location on the medial border of the calcaneus

KID-5 (Shui-quan)

  • Location: 1 cun distal to KID-3, plantar to the distal end of the tibia
  • Notes: Xi-cleft point
  • Indications: Irregular cycling, anestrus, silent heat, dysuria
Anatomical diagram showing KID-5 location plantar to the distal end of the tibia

KID-6 (Zhao-hai)

  • Location: In a depression plantarodistal to the lower border of the medial malleolus
  • Notes: Confluent point with Yin-qiao Extraordinary Channel
  • Indications: Dysuria, constipation, seizure, abnormal cycling, insomnia. Studies indicate stimulation at KID-6 can significantly benefit urination
Anatomical diagram showing KID-6 location in the depression plantarodistal to the medial malleolus

KID-7 (Fu-liu)

  • Location: 2 cun directly proximal to KID-3, on the cranial border of the calcanean tendon
  • Notes: Jing-river (Metal-mother)
  • Indications: Anhidrosis, diarrhea, edema, abdominal fullness, hindlimb paralysis
Anatomical diagram showing KID-7 location on the cranial border of the calcanean tendon

KID-8 (Jiao-xin)

  • Location: 2 cun proximal to KID-3, caudal to the tibia
  • Notes: Xi-cleft point for Yin-qiao Extraordinary Channel
  • Indications: Irregular cycling, hernia, constipation, diarrhea
Anatomical diagram showing KID-8 location caudal to the tibia

KID-9 (Zhu-bin)

  • Location: Cranial to the calcanean tendon, 5 cun dorsal to KID-3, in the medial head of the flexor digitorum profundus m.
  • Notes: Xi-cleft point for Yin-Wei Extraordinary Channel
  • Indications: Hyperactivity, vomiting, hindlimb lameness
Anatomical diagram showing KID-9 location in the medial head of the flexor digitorum profundus muscle

KID-10 (Yin-gu)

  • Location: On the medial side of the popliteal fossa, between the semitendinosus and the gracilis mm.
  • Notes: He-sea point (Water)
  • Indications: Lack of libido, hernia, dysuria, stifle pain
Anatomical diagram showing KID-10 location in the popliteal fossa

KID-27 (Shu-fu)

  • Location: At the level of the manubrium, 2 cun lateral to the midline, between the sternum and the first rib
  • Notes: Association point of all Association points
  • Indications: Cough, heaves, chest pain, vomiting
