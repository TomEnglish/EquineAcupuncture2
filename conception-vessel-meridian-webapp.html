Conception Vessel Meridian (CV)

Overview

  • Origin: The Conception Vessel (Ren Mai) begins at the perineum
  • Trajectory: Runs along the ventral midline of the body through the abdomen and chest
  • Endpoint: Terminates at the lower lip
  • Function: Known as the "Sea of Yin" and governs all Yin meridians, reproductive function, and the anterior-medial aspect of the body
  • Classification: One of the Eight Extraordinary Vessels

CV-1 (Hui-yin)

  • Location: Ventral midline, half-way between the anus and the root of scrotum or vulva. In geldings, is located at the level below the tuber ischium
  • Indications: Infertility, dysuria, seizure, orchitis, scrotal inflammation, vulvar discharge, prolapse of the vagina or uterus
Anatomical diagram showing CV-1 location at the perineum

CV-3 (Zhong-ji)

  • Location: 4 cun caudal to the umbilicus
  • Notes: Alarm point for Bladder
  • Indications: Dysuria, urinary incontinence, lack of libido, hernia, infertility
Anatomical diagram showing CV-3 location on the ventral abdomen

CV-4 (Guan-yuan)

  • Location: 3 cun caudal to umbilicus
  • Notes:
    • Alarm Point for Small Intestine
    • General tonic point
  • Indications: Dysuria, urine retention, infertility, colic diarrhea
Anatomical diagram showing CV-4 location on the ventral abdomen

CV-5 (Shi-men)

  • Location: 2 cun caudal to umbilicus
  • Notes: Alarm Point for Triple Heater
  • Indications: Edema, colic, dysuria, diarrhea
Anatomical diagram showing CV-5 location on the ventral abdomen

CV-8 (Shen-que)

  • Location: At the center of umbilicus
  • Notes: No needles only moxa
  • Indications: Good for foal with diarrhea, chronic fatigue
Anatomical diagram showing CV-8 location at the umbilicus

CV-12 (Zhong-wan)

  • Location: Midway between xiphoid and umbilicus, or 4 cun cranial to umbilicus
  • Notes:
    • Alarm point for Stomach
    • Influential point for Fu organs
  • Indications: Gastric ulcer, diarrhea, jaundice, vomiting
Anatomical diagram showing CV-12 location on the ventral abdomen

CV-14 (Ju-que)

  • Location: At the tip of xiphoid
  • Notes: Alarm point for Heart
  • Indications: Chest pain, palpitation, gastric ulcer
Anatomical diagram showing CV-14 location at the xiphoid

CV-17 (Tan-zhong)

  • Location: At the level of the caudal border of elbow, or on the midline at the level of 4th intercostal space
  • Notes: Alarm point for Pericardium
  • Indications: Heaves, cough, chest pain, vomiting, diaphragm spasm
Anatomical diagram showing CV-17 location on the ventral thorax

CV-22 (Tian-tu)

  • Location: On the midline in a depression cranial to the sternum
  • Indications: Cough, heaves, asthma, sore throat, loss of voice, thyroid problem and diaphragm spasm
Anatomical diagram showing CV-22 location cranial to the sternum

CV-23 (Lian-quan)

  • Location: On the ventral midline of the neck, just in front of laryngeal head
  • Indications: Laryngeal hemiplegia, dysphagia, swelling of tongue
Anatomical diagram showing CV-23 location on the ventral neck

CV-24 (Cheng-jiang)

  • Location: Lower lip on the midline, 1 cun ventral to the rim of the lower lip
  • Indications: Facial paralysis, too much salivation, manic, behavioral problem
Anatomical diagram showing CV-24 location on the lower lip
