Lung Meridian (LU)

Overview

  • Origin: The Lung meridian begins in the middle jiao (stomach)
  • Emergence: Emerges at LU-1 in the center of the pectorals (1st ICS)
  • Trajectory: Travels abaxially to the cubital crease (medial elbow) to LU-5, then follows the craniomedial aspect of the radius, passes caudal to the carpus and metacarpus
  • Endpoint: Terminates just above the coronet, near the cranial edge of the medial collateral cartilage (LU-11)
  • Function: Governs respiration and the dispersing and descending of Qi

LU-1 (Zhong-fu)

  • Location: Depression in the middle of the pectoralis descendens muscle, 1.5 cun lateral to the pectoral sulcus (1st ICS)
  • Notes:
    • "Center of the boob"
    • Alarm point for Lung
  • Indications: Cough, asthma, heaves, shoulder pain, chest pain
Anatomical diagram showing LU-1 location in the pectoral area

LU-5 (Chi-ze)

  • Location: In the depression on the craniomedial aspect of the radius just lateral to the tendon of insertion of the biceps brachii, lateral to cephalic v. in cubital crease
  • Notes:
    • He-sea point (Water)
    • Strum taught biceps tendon to play 5 chi-ze songs
  • Indications: Lung Excess Heat, acute cough, dyspnea, coarse voice, high fever, chest pain, sore throat, pain in shoulder and elbow, diarrhea
Anatomical diagram showing LU-5 location at the cubital crease

LU-6 (Kong-zui)

  • Location: 5 cun distal to LU-5, just caudal to the extensor carpi radialis muscle on the medial aspect of the limb, cranial to cephalic v.
  • Notes: Xi-cleft point for Lung
  • Indications: Acute upper airway inflammation, pharyngitis, pain in shoulder and elbow
Anatomical diagram showing LU-6 location on the medial aspect of the limb

LU-7 (Lie-que)

  • Location: On the craniomedial edge of the radius, just caudal to the extensor carpi radialis tendon, 1.5 cun proximal to the radiocarpal joint at the level of the distal border of the chestnut
  • Notes:
    • Master Point for Head and Neck
    • Luo-connecting point
    • Confluent point with CV
    • Horses often react
    • DV angle of insertion
  • Indications: Cervical stiffness and pain, facial paralysis, cough, heaves
Anatomical diagram showing LU-7 location on the craniomedial edge of the radius

LU-9 (Tai-yuan)

  • Location: On the medial side of the carpus at the level of the articulation between the radius and the radial carpal bone
  • Notes:
    • Use 1 of 9 lives trying not to hit RC jt
    • Shu-stream point (Earth)
    • Yuan primary point
    • Influential point for pulse & vessels
  • Indications: Chronic cough/asthma, heaves, weak voice, pain in carpal joints
Anatomical diagram showing LU-9 location on the medial side of the carpus

LU-10 (Yu-ji)

  • Location: On the medial side of the forelimb, at the level of the midpoint of the third metacarpal (cannon bone), between suspensory ligament and deep digital flexor tendon
  • Notes:
    • Ying-spring point (Fire)
    • Low 4 point, but higher
  • Indications: Lung Heat, fever, sore throat, cough, tendonitis, pain in carpal joints
Anatomical diagram showing LU-10 location on the medial side of the forelimb

LU-11 (Shao-shang)

  • Location: Palmaromedial aspect of the front hoof, 0.5 cun proximal to coronary band, just dorsal to the medial collateral cartilage
  • Notes:
    • Jing-well point (Wood)
    • LI-1 = craniomedial counterpart
  • Indications: Asthma, bleeder, cough, allergy (dust, mold, pollen), nasal discharge, lung problems, fatigue, sole bruising, laminitis
Anatomical diagram showing LU-11 location near the coronary band

Lung Meridian - Five-Element Points

Point Element Type Primary Function
LU-11 Wood Jing-well Powerful for acute conditions
LU-10 Fire Ying-spring Treats Heat conditions
LU-9 Earth Shu-stream Yuan source point
LU-8 Metal Jing-river Treats Qi-level issues
LU-5 Water He-sea Treats chronic conditions

Clinical Applications

  • Respiratory issues: LU-1, LU-5, LU-7, LU-9, LU-11
  • Fever and Heat conditions: LU-5, LU-10
  • Head and neck problems: LU-7
  • Joint pain: LU-5 (elbow), LU-9 (carpus)
  • Allergies: LU-11
