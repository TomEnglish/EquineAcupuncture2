Bladder Meridian (BL)

Overview

  • Origin: The Bladder meridian begins at the medial canthus of the eye (BL-1)
  • Trajectory: Runs up to the earbase (BL-7), the atlas (BL-10) then to the withers (BL-11), then continues down the back in two parallel lines (inner and outer Bladder lines) all the way to the lateral aspect of the hind foot
  • Endpoint: Terminates just proximal to the caudolateral coronet (BL-67)
  • Function: Longest meridian in the body, governs water metabolism and urinary function

BL-1

  • Location: Indentation at the medial canthus
  • Indications: Eye problems, conjunctivitis and uveitis
  • Notes: Contraindication of moxibustion
Anatomical diagram showing BL-1 location at the medial canthus of the eye

BL-2 (Cuan-zhu)

  • Location: In the depression between the dorsal orbital rim and the temporal line of the frontal bone
  • Notes: Leading edge of the eyebrow
  • Indications: Eye problems, conjunctivitis and uveitis
  • Caution: Contraindication of moxibustion
Anatomical diagram showing BL-2 location at the eyebrow area

BL-7 (Tong-tian)

  • Location: 1.5 cun lateral to the dorsocranial aspect of the nuchal crest, between ear and forelock
  • Indications: Nasal discharge and congestion
Anatomical diagram showing BL-7 location between ear and forelock

BL-10 (Tian-zhu)

  • Location: In a depression just caudal to the wings of the atlas, 1.5 cun from the dorsal midline
  • Notes: Back of bridle path
  • Indications: Wind-cold, nasal congestion and discharge, cervical stiffness
Anatomical diagram showing BL-10 location at the atlas area

BL-11 (Da-zhu)

  • Location: Cranial to the withers (at the 2nd thoracic vertebral space), 1.5 cun lateral to the dorsal midline
  • Notes:
    • Ahead of T3 (last palpable thoracic DSP)
    • Influential point for bone (arthritis/disc)
  • Indications: Cough, heaves, fever, cervical stiffness, forelimb lameness
Anatomical diagram showing BL-11 location at the withers area

BL-12 (Feng-men) / BL-41

  • Location: At the 4th thoracic intervertebral space, 1 cun lateral to the dorsal midline. (BL-41 is 5 cun lateral to BL-12)
  • Notes: Influential point for Wind/trachea
  • Indications: Wind-Cold, WindHeat, skin itching, cervical stiffness, cough
Anatomical diagram showing BL-12/BL-41 location at the thoracic area

BL-13 (Fei-shu) / BL-42

  • Location: In the 8th intercostal space, 3 cun lateral to the dorsal midline in the groove between the longissimus and the iliocostalis muscles. (BL-42 is 3 cun lateral to BL-13)
  • Notes:
    • Just behind the angle of the scapula
    • Back-shu association point for LU
  • Indications: Cough, heaves, asthma, tidal fever, sweat, nasal congestion
Anatomical diagram showing BL-13/BL-42 location behind the scapula

BL-23 (Shen-shu) / BL-52

  • Location: 2nd lumbar intervertebral space (L2-L3), 3 cun from the dorsal midline. (BL-52 is 3 cun lateral to BL-23)
  • Notes:

    • Back-shu association point for KID (Traditional association point for BL)
  • Indications: Urinary incontinence, lack of libido, edema, ear problems, back pain
Anatomical diagram showing BL-23/BL-52 location at the lumbar area

BL-28 (Pang-guang-shu)

  • Location: 2nd sacral intervertebral space (S2-S3), 3 cun lateral to the dorsal midline
  • Notes: Back-shu association point for BL
  • Indications: Urinary difficult or incontinence, diarrhea, constipation, back pain
  • Special Use: Moxa for longterm management of urine dribbling
Anatomical diagram showing BL-28 location at the sacral area

BL-40 (Wei-zhong)

  • Location: In the popliteal fossa, between the medial and lateral head of the gastrocnemius m.
  • Notes:
    • Master point for caudal back and hips
    • He-sea point (Earth)
  • Indications: Dysuria, urinary incontinence, hip joint, back pain
Anatomical diagram showing BL-40 location at the popliteal fossa

BL-60 (Kun-lun)

  • Location: Between the lateral malleolus of the tibia and the calcaneal tuber
  • Notes:
    • Follow the rivulet to the web of the hock, proximal to the plantar pouch of the TC joint
    • Opposite KID-3
    • Jing-river (Fire). "Aspirin point"
  • Indications: Cervical stiffness, nose bleeding, seizure, promotion of parturition
  • Caution: Contraindication for pregnancy
Anatomical diagram showing BL-60 location at the hock area

BL-67 (Zhi-yin)

  • Location: Plantarolateral aspect of the pelvic limb, 0.5 cun proximal to the coronary band
  • Notes: Jing-well point (Metal), mother point
  • Indications: Nasal congestion and discharge, nose bleeding, eye problems, promotion of parturition, laminitis
  • Caution: Contraindication for pregnancy
Anatomical diagram showing BL-67 location near the coronary band

Bladder Meridian - Clinical Applications

  • Urinary function: BL-28, BL-23, BL-40
  • Back pain: BL-23, BL-40, BL-60
  • Eye problems: BL-1, BL-2, BL-67
  • Respiratory issues: BL-13, BL-12
  • Pregnancy/Parturition: BL-67, BL-60 (with caution)
  • Cervical stiffness: BL-10, BL-11, BL-60
